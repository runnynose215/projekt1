Vladimir Nabokov's 1930 novel The Defense or Luzhin's Defense

Although lesser known, The Defense by Vladimir Nabokov is equally representative of the author’s theme of the haunted genius. Its protagonist, Aleksandr Ivanovich Luzhin, is a former chess prodigy and Russian émigré living in Europe who is approaching middle age. His obsession with chess helps him escape two major traumatic events in childhood: forced removal from his home country and financial exploitation by a chess manager who abandoned him once his celebrity began to fade. Unfortunately, chess also prevents Luzhin from fully engaging with society in a normal, healthy way. Eventually his inability to escape his childhood horrors and successfully transition into adulthood cause his demise.
